What is Web Accessibility?

Web accessibility means making websites that everyone can use, including people with disabilities.

Simple Accessibility Examples

1. Good Headings

Use headings in order: h1, then h2, then h3.

<h1>Main Title</h1>
<h2>Section Title</h2>
<h3>Subsection Title</h3>

2. Alt Text for Images

Always describe your images for people who can't see them.

<img src="dog.jpg" alt="A happy golden retriever playing in the park">

3. Good Links

Make link text clear and descriptive.

❌ Bad: <a href="#">Click here</a>
✅ Good: <a href="#">Download the HTML guide</a>

4. Form Labels

Always label your form inputs.

<label for="name">Your Name:</label>
<input type="text" id="name" name="name">

Easy Accessibility Tips

  • Use proper HTML tags (header, main, footer)
  • Write good alt text for images
  • Make sure your website works with just a keyboard
  • Use good color contrast
  • Keep your content simple and clear
